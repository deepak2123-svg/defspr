SSB Interview

The Board Does Not
Wait for the Unprepared.

Five days. Four assessors. One recommendation. The SSB is not an exam — it is an observation of who you are. Prepare like it matters.

Day 1 — Screening
OIR Test & PPDT
Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) test — verbal and non-verbal. Picture Perception and Discussion Test (PPDT). Only screened-in candidates proceed to Day 2.
Day 2 — Psychology
Psyche Tests
Thematic Apperception Test (TAT) · Word Association Test (WAT) · Situation Reaction Test (SRT) · Self Description (SD). Assessors observe your Officer Like Qualities through responses.
Day 3–4 — GTO
GTO Tasks
Group Discussion · Group Planning Exercise (GPE) · Progressive Group Task (PGT) · Half Group Task (HGT) · Individual Obstacles · Command Task · Lecturette. Assessed by the Group Testing Officer.
Day 4 — Interview
Personal Interview
One-on-one with the Interviewing Officer (IO). Covers PIQ form, schooling, family background, current affairs, defence knowledge, and self-awareness. No trick questions — just who you are.
Day 5 — Conference
Conference Board
All assessors convene. The President presides. Each officer discusses the candidate. A final Recommended or Not Recommended decision is made and announced on the same day.
Guided Programme
SSB Coaching
Residential & online mock SSBs with retired GTO officers. Personalised feedback, personality development, and conference preparation.

Most frequently cited rejection reasons at Military Hospital Boards. Many are correctable — identify your risks early and act before your SSB date.

Often Correctable — Act Early
Underweight / Overweight
Most common rejection. Diet and structured training 3–6 months before your board.
Dental Points Below 14
See a dentist at least 6 months before. Fillings and extractions count.
Flat Feet (Mild)
Corrective arch exercises help mild pes planus. Severe cases are rejected.
Deviated Nasal Septum (Severe)
Surgical correction is possible — must be done 6+ months before board date.
Varicose Veins
Surgical correction required well in advance. Do not ignore visible leg veins.
Difficult to Correct — Know Early
Vision Beyond Limits
Myopia >3.5D disqualifies for most entries. LASIK disqualifies for Flying Branch.
Colour Blindness (CP-III)
No correction possible. Disqualifying for Flying Branch, Navy Executive, and others.
Hearing Loss >20 dB
Audiometry is conducted. No hearing aids permitted.
Tattoos in Prohibited Areas
Permanent. Face, neck, hands — no exceptions. Plan accordingly before joining.
Cardiac Anomalies
Structural defects, significant arrhythmias on ECG — disqualifying.

Common Questions

FAQ

Quick answers to questions every aspirant asks.

  • Yes. Candidates appearing in Class 12 or equivalent examination are eligible to apply. However, you must pass the 12th board before joining NDA. Navy and Air Force wings additionally require Physics and Mathematics at 12th level.
  • Yes. Following the Supreme Court order in September 2022, female candidates are eligible for NDA examination. Women can apply for all three wings — Army, Navy, and Air Force — subject to meeting the educational and medical requirements.
  • For NDA I 2026 (exam date 12 Apr 2026), candidates must be born between 2 July 2007 and 1 July 2010 — i.e., between 16.5 and 19.5 years of age on the date of commencement of the course (January 2027). Use our eligibility calculator above for your exact window.
  • There is no specified limit on the number of attempts. You can appear in any NDA examination as long as you meet the age and educational eligibility criteria. Since NDA is conducted twice a year and the age window is 3 years, most candidates get 4–6 attempts total.
  • NDA Written Exam has two papers: Paper I — Mathematics (300 marks, 2.5 hours) and Paper II — General Ability Test or GAT (600 marks, 2.5 hours). Total written marks: 900. Negative marking of ⅓ mark per wrong answer applies. SSB Interview is 900 marks. Grand total: 1800 marks.
  • The SSB is a 5-day personality and intelligence assessment. Day 1–2: Stage 1 — Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) test and Picture Perception & Discussion Test (PPDT). If cleared, Stage 2 begins: Psychological Tests (TAT, WAT, SRT, SD), Group Testing Officer tasks (GTO), Personal Interview, and final Conference Board.
  • For Army and Air Force Ground Duty branches, spectacles are permitted within limits (myopia up to −3.5D, hyperopia up to +3.5D). For Air Force Flying Branch, perfect unaided 6/6 vision in both eyes is required — no spectacles, and LASIK surgery also disqualifies. Navy standards vary by branch.
  • No. Any graduate can apply for AFCAT for Ground Duty (Non-Technical) branch. Engineering graduates can additionally apply for Ground Duty (Technical) branch. Flying branch requires graduation with Physics and Mathematics at 10+2 level, plus minimum 60% in both subjects.
